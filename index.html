Academic projects

Gamification Prototype for Virtual Teams

For my Master's Thesis, I explored how competitive gamification can enhance engagement and collaboration within virtual teams.

Traditional security training is often perceived as dry and unengaging. To address this, I developed a Unity-based virtual office simulation where remote team members collaborated to learn and execute cybersecurity protocols.

The prototype integrated competitive elements like leaderboards and NPCs to drive behavior. Through experimental research, I demonstrated that this approach reduced the psychological distance between team members and significantly increased engagement with the training material compared to traditional methods.

Smart Light App — UX Design

My study group and I redesigned the SG Smart lighting application to improve its usability and user experience.

Through surveys, interviews, and usability testing (including the System Usability Scale), we identified key areas for improvement, then developed iterative prototypes in Figma and Unity — simplifying navigation, improving visual hierarchy, and creating intuitive iconography.

The final prototype significantly increased usability scores and received positive user feedback.
