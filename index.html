Smart Light App UX Design

For this project, my study group and I redesigned the SG Smart lighting application to enhance its usability and user experience.

By conducting user research through surveys, interviews, and usability testing with methods like the System Usability Scale, we identified key areas for improvement.

We then developed iterative prototypes using Figma and Unity, focusing on simplifying navigation, improving visual hierarchy, and creating intuitive iconography.

Through this user-centered design approach, the final prototype significantly increased usability scores and received positive user feedback, demonstrating a substantial improvement in user satisfaction.

Life Below Water: Spreading Awareness Through a Serious Game
For this project, my team and I developed a Unity-based 3D platformer aimed at children aged 9 to 12, focusing on the United Nations' "Life Below Water" global goal.

By conducting workshops with schoolchildren, we created an engaging game that educates players about marine conservation and the impacts of pollution.

Castle Runners
In this project, we developed "Castle Runners," an endless runner and puzzle game that enhances player engagement through webcam-based head tracking.

By integrating computer vision techniques, players control the game using physical head movements, increasing accessibility without the need for specialized hardware.

Through user testing, we demonstrated that the head-tracking control scheme significantly improves player engagement compared to traditional controls. A preview of the development process can be seen in the video to the right.
